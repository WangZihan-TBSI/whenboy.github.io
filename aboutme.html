About Me

Dr. Wenbo Ding is now a postdoctoral research fellow in Materials Science and Engineering at Georgia Tech, Atlanta, GA, working with Professor Z. L. Wang. He received the B.E and Ph.D. degrees (both with the highest honors) from the Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University, Beijing, China, in 2011 and 2016, respectively. He has published over 60 journal and conference papers and received many prestigious awards, including the IEEE Scott Helt Memorial Award for the best paper published in IEEE Transactions on Broadcasting, the Tsinghua Top Grade Scholarship (highest student honor in Tsinghua), the Excellent Ph.D. Graduate of Beijing City, the Excellent Doctoral Dissertation of Tsinghua University, the 1st place in Georgia Tech MSE Poster Competition, the National Graduate Scholarship. He has been the reviewer for many top journals and conferences, including Advanced Materials, Nano Today, Nano Energy, IEEE TSP/TCOM/JSAC/TWC/TBC/TVT/PJ and etc. and been the TPC member or organizer for many IEEE conferences. His current research interests include flexible and self-powered sensors, mechanical energy harvesting for IoT and IoUT, human-machine interface and cyber security with the help of advanced signal processing, machine learning, and communication theory.
